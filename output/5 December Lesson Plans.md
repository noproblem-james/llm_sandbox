# Language Arts

 
## Holiday Storytelling

### Have each student create a story about a holiday tradition from a different country.

### Time Estimate

Approximately 45 minutes.

### List of Materials

- Pen and paper for each student
- Access to a computer with internet connection
- Books or other resources about holiday traditions from different countries

### Procedure

1. Introduce the activity by explaining that each student will be creating a story about a holiday tradition from a different country.
2. Ask each student to choose a country and research a holiday tradition from that country.
3. Provide students with access to books or other resources about holiday traditions from different countries.
4. Ask students to use the information they have gathered to create a story about the holiday tradition they have chosen.
5. Once students have finished their stories, have them share them with the rest of the class.

### Additional Notes

- Encourage students to be creative and imaginative in their stories.
- Ask students to use descriptive language and to include details about the holiday tradition they have chosen.
- Ask students to include a moral or lesson in their stories.

### Differentiation Strategies

- For students who need more support, provide them with a list of questions to help them brainstorm ideas for their stories.
- For students who need more challenge, ask them to create a story that includes multiple holiday traditions from different countries.
- For neurodivergent students, provide them with a visual representation of the story structure to help them organize their thoughts.

 
Good luck!

# Math

 
## Snowflake Symmetry Art

### Create a symmetrical snowflake art project using paper and scissors.

### Time Estimate

Approximately 45-60 minutes

### List of Materials

- Paper
- Scissors
- Colored pencils or markers (optional)

### Procedure

1. Explain the concept of symmetry to the students.
2. Give each student a piece of paper and have them fold it in half.
3. Have the students draw a symmetrical snowflake on the paper, using the fold as the center line.
4. Have the students cut out their snowflakes.
5. If desired, have the students color their snowflakes with colored pencils or markers.

### Additional Notes

- Encourage the students to use creative designs for their snowflakes.
- Demonstrate how to draw and cut out a snowflake, if needed.

### Differentiation strategies

- For students who need more support: Provide pre-drawn snowflake templates for students to use as a starting point.
- For students who need more challenge: Encourage students to create more complex snowflake designs.
- For neurodivergent students: Allow students to use a ruler or other straight edge to help draw and cut out their snowflakes.

## Winter Holidays Around the World

### Research and discuss different winter holidays celebrated around the world.

This lesson will help second graders explore and discuss different winter holidays celebrated around the world. Students will research different winter holidays and their traditions, and then discuss their findings with their classmates.

### Time Estimate

This lesson will take approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes.

### List of Materials

- Computers or tablets with internet access
- Paper and pencils

### Procedure

1. Introduce the lesson and explain the goal of researching and discussing different winter holidays celebrated around the world.

2. Divide the class into small groups of two or three students.

3. Have each group research a different winter holiday and its traditions.

4. After researching, have each group present their findings to the class.

5. After all groups have presented, have the class discuss the different winter holidays and their traditions.

### Additional Notes

- Encourage students to use reliable sources for their research, such as the library or online encyclopedias.
- Ask students to think of questions they would like to ask their classmates about their holiday.
- Have students draw pictures of the winter holidays they researched.

### Differentiation strategies

- For students who need more support: Provide a list of reliable sources for them to use for their research.

- For students who need more challenge: Ask them to research more than one holiday and compare and contrast their traditions.

- For neurodivergent students: Allow them to work independently or in a small group and provide them with additional time to research and present.

# Science

 
## Winter Animal Hide-and-Seek

### Students take turns hiding and seeking winter animals, while learning about their adaptations.

### Time Estimate

Approximately 1 hour

### List of Materials

- Winter animal figures
- Winter animal cards
- Outdoor space

### Procedure

1. Introduce the activity to the students by explaining that they will be taking turns hiding and seeking winter animals.
2. Divide the students into two teams.
3. Give each team a set of winter animal figures and cards.
4. Explain the rules of the game:
- Each team will take turns hiding and seeking winter animals.
- The team that finds the most animals wins.
5. Allow each team to take turns hiding and seeking winter animals.
6. As the students play, discuss the adaptations of the winter animals.
7. When the game is over, have each team tally up the number of animals they found.
8. Declare the winning team.

### Additional Notes

- Encourage the students to be creative with their hiding spots.
- Ask questions about the animals' adaptations to help the students think critically about the game.
- Remind the students to be respectful of the environment and to put the animals back where they found them.

### Differentiation strategies

- For students who need more support:
- Provide additional support by having an adult or older student accompany the team.
- Provide a set of winter animal figures and cards that are easier to identify.
- For students who need more challenge:
- Ask the students to identify the animals by their scientific names.
- Ask the students to explain the adaptations of the winter animals.
- For neurodivergent students:
- Allow the students to play the game in a smaller group.
- Allow the students to take breaks as needed.

## Winter Animal Bingo

### Students play bingo while learning about winter animals and their adaptations.

### Time Estimate
This activity will take approximately 45 minutes to complete.

### List of Materials
- Bingo cards (1 for each student)
- Markers or chips to mark the bingo cards
- Animal cards with pictures and information (1 card for each student)

### Procedure
1. Introduce the lesson by discussing the different animals that live in the winter and the adaptations they have to survive the cold temperatures.
2. Distribute the bingo cards and animal cards to each student.
3. Explain the rules of bingo and how to play.
4. Call out the names of the animals and their adaptations and have the students mark their cards.
5. The first student to get a bingo is the winner!

### Additional Notes
- The teacher should provide examples of the animals and their adaptations to help the students understand.
- Encourage students to think about how the animals are adapted to survive the cold temperatures.

### Differentiation strategies
- For students who need more support: Provide additional examples of the animals and their adaptations.
- For students who need more challenge: Ask them to explain how the animals are adapted to survive the cold temperatures.
- For neurodivergent students: Provide visuals of the animals and their adaptations to help them understand.
